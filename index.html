2. D3 is really helpful in multiple ways. Some of the key ways are its physics engine,
its scaling, and flexibility in style/graphing ability. The physics engine helps us see correlation
between nodes and its links (as shown in my first graph). The scaling is great because you can color coordinate
based on somethings "influence" and adjust how influential the number scaling and color changing is. Finally
There is so many libraries to choose from and helper functions to make the process even easier. You can create so many types of graphs.

3. It is not the most optimal for dealing with large datasets. When I process 1000 points for the first
infographic, there is lag. With more points, even more lag. The learning curve is very steep.
Figuring out what all of these functions do and why they do what they do was very confusing for me.
It would be even worse for a new engineer. Lastly, you're limited to D3. Although it is very expansive of a library,
There is still potential infographics that could be displayed better or physics engines that one could implement themselves to show unique relationships.

4. In “A Comprehensive Guide to Accessible Data Visualization,” the article talks about the autonomy
of a accesible graph. One of the imporant things that it mentions is that color scheme should be accessible
to color blind people. In fact, color is not nessesary in my infographics, only the darkness of the color,
which can be detected by colorblind people. However, some of my font is small, which is not as accessible to someone
who is hard of seeing. A change I could make going forward is to give larger text for the infographic, but
also add blurbs that are large in font for people to catch the graphic quickly withe ease.

5. Like I said earlier, there should be a start with a focus not on actual color itself. For example, don't
rely on color coding, rather use color for scaling. Make things very visible to the hard of seeing when planning.
For the neurological accesiblity, adding ways to make information consise and simple is also important.
Think about all kinds of audiences when designing the infographic.
